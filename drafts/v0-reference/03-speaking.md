There is a thing people do, once they have learned the shape of NVC, where they use the shape to deliver the same complaints they were already delivering, in a tone that suggests they have done the reading. The grievance gets a clean coat of paint. Nothing else changes. The other person, who has not done the reading, feels the indictment under the careful grammar and braces accordingly. Now there are two people in a room: one wielding a method, one suspecting they are being managed. Paperwork with feelings on it.

The shape itself is fine. Four moves. An observation — what specifically happened, said the way a security camera would say it, no adjectives smuggled in. A feeling — yours, body-located, not an opinion about the other person dressed in a feeling word. A need — the human-universal thing the feeling is pointing at. A request — something doable, in the next minute or the next week, that the other person can say no to without the world ending.

Observation. Feeling. Need. Request. OFNR if you like acronyms. People who like acronyms tend to use them as armor. Try not to.

The trouble is that the four moves can be performed without the one thing that makes them matter, which is curiosity about the person on the other side. You can OFNR someone into a wall. You can deliver a flawless one and be doing nothing but reading them their charges. The structure is an instrument. Whether it makes music or noise depends on what you are actually trying to do. Any tool can be turned into a weapon; we'll let that sentence sit there and move on.

The closest thing to an antidote is the move toward what you are longing for instead of what you are sick of. *I find myself longing for more of our evenings to have some ease in them. Some of the old playfulness.* Not a complaint flipped inside out. An articulation of what you are reaching toward, said plainly enough that you can hear yourself wanting it. Complaining is a known shape. Wanting is exposing.

A few things to watch for. The robot problem: OFNR delivered with such deliberate scaffolding that the other person feels processed. The goal is connection, not compliance. There is the move where you start fighting over who is doing NVC correctly — a fight that has nothing to do with NVC and everything to do with wanting to be right. And there is structure-as-weapon: immaculate language used to corner someone. The form is intact. The intent is hostile. The other person feels it in their shoulders.

One aside. Whatever observation you are about to deliver, you have been wrong about before. Often enough that holding it loosely is more honest than holding it tightly.

**She raises her voice on the phone with her mother and then says, *I feel like you're always trying to make me the bad guy here.***
That is a thought wearing a feeling's clothes. Underneath: her mother said *well, if that's how you want to remember it.* She felt small and a little furious. She is longing for her own version of the past to be treated as real. What she could ask for: *Could you tell me what you remember, without telling me what I should remember?*

**Across the kitchen, after the third unanswered text about the leaking sink: *you never fucking care about anything I do around here.***
The sentence is doing a job. It is not a precise job. Underneath the heat: *I have been carrying this and I do not feel seen carrying it.* The need is mattering. Being someone whose effort registers. That need has to be honored first, by the speaker, in private, so the next sentence is not another swing. Then, maybe: *I've been holding the house stuff for two weeks and I'm exhausted and I want to feel like we're actually doing this together.* Same fire. Aimed somewhere it can land.

**The longing version, without the saccharine.**
*I keep bringing up the dishes and I don't want to be the dishes person in this relationship. What I want is the feeling we had on Sunday mornings, where neither of us was keeping score. I'm not sure how to get back there. I wanted to say it out loud.*

**The weaponized OFNR, delivered in a voice trained to sound calm.**
*When you came home at 7:42 last Tuesday, I felt disrespected, because I have a need for consideration, and I'd like to request that you commit to a specific arrival time going forward.* Every box checked. Nothing alive in the room. No curiosity about what was happening for the other person, no softness in the asking, no acknowledgment that *disrespected* is a verdict, not a feeling. The form is doing the work the heart was supposed to do.

**The robot problem, in miniature.**
*I am observing that you are loading the dishwasher in a manner I am experiencing as effortful to witness.* Your partner is not going to fall in love with you again hearing this. The point was never the sentence. The point was the person.
